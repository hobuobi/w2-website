Sexual Assault

>> SHORT TERM <<

Reward Student Groups Helping the Cause. Let’s give more UC money to student groups that help to fight sexual assault, including OSAPR affirmative consent and bystander training. Most students are in extracurricular clubs, and the UC funds most events they throw. By reaching most of campus, we can have a positive at a huge scale. The best part? We don’t need the Administration’s approval, just a commitment from the UC and student leaders.

>> MID TERM <<

Get Student Organizations on Board with Safe Party Guidelines. Parties are better when students are safer. We plan to create a task force with voices from the UC and student organizations to agree on suggested guidelines for safer on-campus parties. An example could be having posters up at each party with the faces, names, and contact info of point people who students can contact if something doesn’t feel right. With these guidelines, let’s encourage each organization to look out for its own community.

>> LONG TERM <<

Improve Campus Culture, Starting with the Housing Culture. Don’t let the conversation about sexual assault end at your first year on campus. There are so many orientations freshman year, that it’s difficult to make anything stick. The conversation shouldn’t stop at freshman year. Every year, let’s allow sophomores to move in early to meet each other, learn community values, and receive an affirmative consent orientation.
